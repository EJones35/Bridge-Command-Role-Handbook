Captain

Home

Main Role

The captain is the role with the highest say on the ship. However, this does not mean they have absolute power - that is what the detention bay is for. The captain should:

  1. Listen to the crew
  2. Delegate tasks
  3. Remember key information
  4. Coordinate crew actions
  5. Make final decisions
  6. Communicate mission priorities
  7. Maintain crew morale
  8. Give everyone a chance
  9. Encourage new players

The captain should not:

  1. Take over a station without permission
  2. Ruin someone else's time - they paid the same money you did for the experience
  3. Dominate every conversation

Crew Subteam

The captain is part of the command subteam.

The command subteam ensures that every station is working toward the same objective, even when conditions are chaotic and information is incomplete.

Location

Havock

On the Havock, the captain sits in the centre of the bridge, semi-surrounded by railings. The helm officer is in front of them.

Takanami

On the Takanami, the captain sits in the centre of the bridge, with no railings. The helm and navigation officers are in front of them.

Skills Required

  • Leadership
  • Communication
  • Decision making
  • Remaining calm under pressure
  • Understanding other stations

Callouts

Wrong: "Someone fix engineering!"

Right: "Engineering, reactor temperature is increasing. Please investigate and report."

Wrong: "Helm, turn to 83°, no, too far and put impulse on 100%..."

Right: "Helm, take us to that station."

Common Mistakes

  • Trying to control every station
  • Giving unclear orders
  • Forgetting to listen to specialists
  • Making decisions without enough information

Advanced Tips

  • Trust your officers
  • Keep communication concise
  • Think several steps ahead
  • Let specialists do their jobs

Station

As the captain, you can't change anything on your station, but you do have:

  • Ship Log
  • Database
  • Alert Level Control
  • Tactical Radar
  • Long Range Radar

Ship Log

The ship log is a log of most things that happens on the ship, for example, communications.

Database

The database contains information about the world you're in. In some missions, data may be added that you need to look though.

Alert Level Controls

The alert level controller is used to control the alert level on the ship. This is shown by the light colours.

Tactical Radar

The tactical radar has the same range of sight as helm and weapons (5 units).

Long Range Radar

The long range radar has the same range of sight as science (30 units).

Emergency Procedures

  • Assess the situation
  • Assign priorities
  • Coordinate stations
  • Communicate clearly

First Officer (XO)

Home

Main Role

The first officer is the role with the second highest say on the ship. The first officer should:

  1. Listen to the captain
  2. Make sure the crew knows their jobs
  3. Inform the captain
  4. Captain the shuttle
  5. Understand what needs to happen

The first officer should not:

  1. Take over a station without permission
  2. Ruin someone else's time - they paid the same money you did for the experience
  3. Dominate every conversation
  4. Go against the captain except in extreme circumstances.

Crew Subteam

The first officer is part of the command subteam.

The command subteam ensures that every station is working toward the same objective, even when conditions are chaotic and information is incomplete.

Location

Havock

On the Havock, the first officer does not have a space for a station. This may be replaced by standing next to the captain's station. On the Havock shuttle, the first officer would normally sit at the front.

Takanami

On the Takanami, the first officer does not have a space for a station. This may be replaced by standing next to the captain's station. On the Takanami shuttle, the first officer would normally sit at the front.

Skills Required

  • Leadership
  • Communication
  • Decision making
  • Remaining calm under pressure
  • Understanding other stations

Callouts

Wrong: "Captain, you're wrong."

Right: "Captain, you can't fire a nuke at them, they're our ally."

Wrong: "Captain, I'm taking the shuttle."

Right: "Captain, permission to deploy the shuttle? Ok shuttle crew, let's move."

Common Mistakes

  • Trying to control every station
  • Giving unclear orders
  • Going against the captain
  • Forgetting to listen to specialists

Advanced Tips

  • Trust your captain
  • Keep communication concise
  • Think several steps ahead
  • Let specialists do their jobs

Station

As the fist officer, you do not have a designated station. However, on the shuttle, you may be in charge of shuttle comms to the main ship and flight controller.

Helm

Home

Main Role

The helm officer is the person driving the ship. The helm officer should:

  1. Listen to the captain
  2. Listen to the engineers
  3. Understand target priority
  4. Follow waypoints
  5. Avoid crashing

The helm officer should not:

  1. Take over a station without permission
  2. Ruin someone else's time - they paid the same money you did for the experience
  3. Dominate every conversation

Crew Subteam

The helm officer is part of the operations subteam.

It is here that intent becomes action. The ship closes distance, avoids danger, brings weapons to bear, shields itself from harm, and applies force where it will matter most.

Location

Havock

On the Havock, the helm officer sits at the front of the bridge. The captain is behind them, the main screen in front.

Takanami

On the Takanami, the helm officer sits at the front of the bridge. The captain is behind them, the main screen in front.

Skills Required

  • Quick reaction
  • Communication
  • Remaining calm under pressure
  • Understanding other stations

Callouts

Wrong: "Place a waypoint."

Right: "Navigation, can I have a waypoint for the station?"

Wrong: "Warping."

Right: "Engineering, can we go at warp?"

Common Mistakes

  • Giving unclear requests
  • Going against command because "you know better".
  • Making important decisions without command permission

Advanced Tips

  • Let the navigation officer map a route if needed e.g. through an astroid belt
  • Keep communication concise
  • Think several steps ahead

Station

As the helm officer, you have access to:

  • Impulse Control
  • Warp Control
  • Tactical Radar

Impulse Control

The impulse control is a slider that goes from -100% to 100%. 100% is full speed ahead, -100% is full speed reverse.

Tactical Radar

The tactical radar has a 5U range of sight.

Emergency Procedures

  • Assess the situation
  • Assign target priorities
  • Communicate clearly

Navigation

Home

Main Role

The navigation officer manages routes between 2 destinations. The navigation officer should:

  1. Listen to the captain
  2. Listen to the helm officer
  3. Understand the ship
  4. Set waypoints

The navigation officer should not:

  1. Take over a station without permission
  2. Ruin someone else's time - they paid the same money you did for the experience
  3. Dominate every conversation

Crew Subteam

The navigation officer is part of the science subteam.

Science provides the ship’s understanding of the battlespace. It gathers, interprets, and distributes information, ensuring that decisions are made with awareness rather than assumption.

Location

Havock

On the Havock, the navigation officer stands at the back left of the bridge. The radar is in front of them, the captain to their left.

Takanami

On the Takanami, the navigation officer sits at the front of the bridge, next to the helm officer. The captain is behind them, the main screen in front.

Skills Required

  • Undersanding of the ship
  • Communication
  • Remaining calm under pressure

Callouts

Wrong: "Follow the waypoint"

Right: "Helm, get to that waypoint"

Wrong: "NO! GO THAT WAY!"

Right: "Helm, I've set you a clear path."

Common Mistakes

  • Giving unclear requests
  • Going against command because "you know better".
  • Trying to force helm to follow a path
  • Making important decisions without command permission

Advanced Tips

  • Let the helm officer get to the waypoints as they wish, unless there is danger in their path
  • Keep communication concise
  • Think several steps ahead

Station

As the navigation officer, you have access to:

  • Waypoints

Waypoints

You have access to 8 colours of 10 waypoints. Use these to map points for helm.

Emergency Procedures

  • Assess the situation
  • Assign target priorities
  • Communicate clearly

This is a fan-made handbook for the roles at Bridge Command. Nothing should be taken as reality. If you don't want to play the way this handbook shows, then don't so long as it doesn't go against basic human respect. Things you may get kicked out for:

  • Disrespecting someone
  • Homophobic, sexist or gender-discriminatory behaviours or comments
  • Swearing
  • Inappropriate body language
  • Intoxication by alcohol or the influence of prohibited substances, recreational or social drugs
  • Threatening people with intent
  • Belittling and demeaning language or behaviours
  • Racist or religious taunts or incitement

Things you shouldn't do on-mission, regardless of role:

  • Take over a station without permission
  • Ruin someone else's time - they paid the same money you did for the experience
  • Dominate every conversation

This is not an exhaustive list but is part of basic human decency.

This information is correct as far as I know. If it isn't, contact "Cadet Jones" on the Bridge Command Discord.
