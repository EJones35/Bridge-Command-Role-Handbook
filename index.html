Captain

Main Role

The Captain is responsible for the strategic direction, safety, and purpose of the vessel. The Captain does not operate systems, execute routine manoeuvres, or personally manage every tactical detail.

Their role is to determine what the ship is trying to achieve, why it matters, and when priorities must change

Priority is therefore:

  1. Preserve the crew
  2. Preserve the vessel
  3. Complete the mission
These priorities are not decorative. Mission success is meaningless if the crew is lost unnecessarily, and a destroyed vessel rarely completes its objective with much dignity.

The Captain’s task is to maintain enough awareness to make sound decisions, while allowing trained officers to act within their areas of responsibility.

At its simplest: The Captain sets intent, the crew executes it.

The Captain is responsible for:

  • Remembering mission objectives and priorities
  • Maintaining overall situational awareness
  • Making decisions when priorities conflict
  • Ensuring the bridge remains coordinated and disciplined
  • Receiving, filtering, and acting on key information
  • Authorising major changes of plan
  • Deciding when to engage, disengage, retreat, negotiate, or escalate
  • Ensuring the crew understands the current intent
A Captain should not attempt to know every detail at all times. That is why the ship has officers.
